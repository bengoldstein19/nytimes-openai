

Kirstie Alley, an Emmy-winning actress best known for her roles on the sitcoms “Cheers” and “Veronica’s Closet,” has died at the age of 71.

Alley was born in Wichita, Kansas, and began her career as a dancer on Broadway. She made her television debut in the 1981 miniseries “Masquerade” and went on to star in the sitcoms “Cheers” and “Veronica’s Closet.” She won two Emmys for her role as Rebecca Howe on “Cheers” and was nominated for a Golden Globe for her role on “Veronica’s Closet.”

Alley also appeared in several films, including “Look Who’s Talking” and its sequels, “Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan,” and “It Takes Two.” She also starred on the reality show “Kirstie Alley’s Big Life.”

Alley is survived by her two children, William True and Lillie Price.
