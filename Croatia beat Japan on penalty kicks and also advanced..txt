

Croatia beat Japan on penalty kicks in the Round of 16 of the FIFA World Cup, advancing to the quarterfinals for the first time in the nation's history.

In a dramatic match that went to extra time, Croatia faced off against Japan in Rostov-on-Don, Russia. Japan had the upper hand for much of the match, controlling possession and creating more chances. However, Croatia’s defense held strong, and the match ended in a 1-1 draw after 90 minutes.

In extra time, both teams had chances to take the lead, but neither could find the back of the net. The match then went to penalty kicks, where Croatia prevailed 3-2.

The win was a historic moment for Croatia, who had never made it past the Round of 16 in their previous three World Cup appearances. Croatia will now face off against host nation Russia in the quarterfinals, a match that will surely draw huge interest from the Croatian public.
